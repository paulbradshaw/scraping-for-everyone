Meet our Governing Body members

All of our Governing Body members have lots of experience and expertise in their different roles, and they also have a real interest in making sure that healthcare services are doing their best for local people.

All voting members of the Governing Body and those employed by the CCG are required to complete statutory and mandatory training, which includes safeguarding children and adults, information governance, and equality and diversity.


Find out more about each of them using the list that follows.

Dr Gora Bangi, Chair and Clinical Leader

Following a successful business career, Gora qualified as a doctor from Newcastle University in 2000.  After initial training in surgery he trained to become a GP, completing his training in 2005 and joining a large practice in Leyland as a partner. He subsequently went on to open the first new surgery in Leyland in over thirty years.

He has taken on many managerial and leadership roles, including practice based commissioning and also as Chair of Central Lancashire Primary Care Trust’s Professional Executive Committee.

He is a GP champion for the Department of Health, a trainer and academic adviser at the University of Manchester, Chair of the North West Advancing Quality Board, and was recently elected to the national Board of NHS Clinical Commissioners.

Dr Richard Kelsall, GP Director

For the last 18 years Richard has been working as a GP at Worden Medical Centre in Leyland.

After graduating from Liverpool University he trained in general surgery and gained a Fellowship of the Royal College of Surgeons in London. He then trained in general practice where he obtained membership of the Royal College of General Practitioners, although he continues to have a special interest in minor surgery within the community.

Three years as a practice based commissioning role led to him becoming involved with Chorley and South Ribble CCG.


Back to the list at the top of the page.

 

Dr Satyendra Singh, GP Director

Satyendra has been a principle GP at Claytonbrook surgery since 2006.

After graduating in India, he spent 20 years working in a variety of hospitals focusing on trauma and orthopaedics, and he has a keen interest in mental health and musculoskeletal issues.

 

 

 

Back to the list at the top of the page.

 

 

Dr Matthew Orr, GP Director

Dr Orr undertook a BSc in Medical Science at the University of St Andrews before qualifying as a doctor from Manchester University in 2000 with his postgraduate GP training undertaken in the Chorley & Preston area. 

Since qualifying as a GP in 2004 he has worked as a partner at a local practice in Withnell. As well as his work as a GP he has several other roles including advising the Care Quality Commission (CQC) during inspections of GP practices, working as a medical adviser to various adoption and fostering panels and sitting on the local Individual Funding Request Panel.

 

Back to the list at the top of the page

 

Dr John Cairns

Dr Cairns graduated in 1997 from Manchester, and has been a full time GP at Library House Surgery in Chorley since 2006.

After gaining a range of commissioning experience working with Central Lancashire Primary Care Trust, where he chaired the local medicines management committee, he currently provides clinical leadership for prescribing within the CCG.  He has also been his surgery’s lead for prescribing for the past eight years.

In 2012 he completed an MSc in the History of Science, Technology and Medicine, focusing on medicalisation and the social construction of illness.

 

Back to the list at the top of the page.

 

Dr Joegy Shah, GP Director

For over 20 years Joegy has been a GP Partner at the Ryan Medical Centre in Bamber Bridge. Brought up in south Manchester, he did his medical training at St Andrews and Manchester University Medical Schools, before finally completing his GP training in Blackburn.

Joegy became a member of the Royal College of General Practitioners (RCGP) in 1993 and has been a GP appraiser for nearly 10 years. He has always enjoyed teaching and being involved in medical undergraduate education and has trained GPs for more than 10 years.  

 

Back to the list at the top of the page.

 

Anthony Gick, Vice Chair and Lay Member for Governance

Anthony has had a lengthy career in health service management, working mainly in Greater Manchester and the North West, spending almost 10 years as Chief Executive of South Lancashire Health Authority.

He is a former chairman of the Lancashire Drug Action Team, and was also Vice Chairman of the NHS Pension Agency Special Health Authority.

Anthony has a law degree from Cambridge University and a Diploma in Health Service Management.

 

Back to the list at the top of the page.

 

 

Linda Chivers, Lay Member for Finance and Audit

Linda is currently Chief Executive of Age Concern Central Lancashire, a post she has been in since 1997.  She is a chartered management accountant with many years of experience working in the not-for-profit and service industries.

During her time with Age Concern Central Lancashire she has been actively involved in developing collaborative approaches to working, and was a Non-Executive Director of Age Concern Support Services (North West) and Age Concern Enterprises Ltd.

 

Back to the list at the top of the page.

 

Geoffrey O'Donoghue, Lay Member for Patient and Public Involvement

Geoffrey has vast experience of working with socially excluded groups and currently works for a Lancashire-based service offering information and advice to adults with low skills levels to help them find learning and work opportunities.

He has hands on experience of service design, strategic planning, and has a sound understanding of regeneration and the role of the community and voluntary sectors.

 


Back to the list at the top of the page.

 

Anne Bowen, Governing Body Nurse

Anne qualified at the Sheffield School of Nursing in 1978 and moved to Preston where she worked in a variety of nursing roles.

An experienced nurse, she continued to develop professionally and clinically achieving her BSc Hons Nurse Practitioner degree and working in senior clinical and managerial roles across both hospital sites of Lancashire Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust.

 


Back to the list at the top of the page.

 

Dr Stephen Cairns, Secondary Care Doctor

Born in Preston, Stephen came back as to the area as a consultant physician to Preston and Chorley Hospitals in 1982 having qualified in London and trained in Southampton, Bristol and Manchester.

Stephen retired in 2010, although he retains a very active interest in the health of the NHS and its future development.

 

 

Back to the list at the top of the page.

 

Jan Ledward, Chief Officer

Jan joined NHS Central Lancashire in 2009 as director of procurement, contracting and market management from NHS Blackburn with Darwen, where she was director of commissioning and performance for seven years. For the last two years of this she was on secondment to NHS North West as lead director for the advancing quality programme.

Jan has worked in the NHS since 1985 and has a professional background in procurement and contract management.


 
 
Back to the list at the top of the page.

 

Matt Gaunt, Chief Finance and Contracting Officer

Matt is responsible for the financial leadership of the organisation, maintaining focus on its purpose and vision through rigorous analysis and challenge. He leads the finance team to ensure the development of medium and long term financial plans to support the organisation’s plans and strategies and the annual budgeting process to ensure financial balance for the organisation.

 

 

 

Back to the list at the top of the page.
 

 

Helen Curtis, Head of Quality and Performance

Helen joined the NHS in 1982 via the National Administrative Training Scheme, having graduated in Government, Politics and Modern History.

Before joining the CCG, Helen was Governance Director at Pennine Acute Hospitals NHS Trust where she was responsible for the Quality Strategy of the organisation, Patient Safety, Clinical Effectiveness, Risk Management, Corporate Governance and Health and Safety.

Helen’s focus on patient safety followed completion of the Patient Safety Officer Executive Development Programme at the Institute for Healthcare Improvement (IHI) in Boston in 2008. Following that, she led the team from Pennine Acute that participated in the highly successful AQuA North West Reducing Mortality Collaborative.

 

Back to the list at the top of the page.
 

 

Jayne Mellor, Head of Planning and Delivery  

Jayne has extensive experience of commissioning and procurement in health and social care. She joined the CCGs in December 2015, having previously worked as associate director within the NHS with the lead for strategy and adults integrated commissioning. Prior to that, Jayne spent 11 years at Lancashire County Council in contracting and procurement roles, and for the last 5 years within the council, as head of commissioning for East Lancashire. 

 

 

 

Back to the list at the top of the page.

 

 

Karen Sharrocks, Head of Strategy and Corporate Services

Karen has worked in and around the NHS for 24 years, and prior to joining the CCG she worked for KPMG LLP within their public sector practice.

Before joining KPMG in 2011 Karen worked in the NHS in various finance, audit, corporate services and project management related roles in a number of  NHS acute and mental health providers across the North West and Staffordshire,  and more recently in commissioning.

Karen is a Chartered Internal Auditor and has an MSc in Audit Management and Consultancy.
